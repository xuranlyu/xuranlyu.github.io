## Current Focus: Rough Volatility

My main research project concerns numerical methods for the rough Heston model. Specifically, I am developing high-order schemes for the **fractional Riccati equation** that arises in the calibration of rough volatility models, with attention to the **asymptotic regimes** that govern long- and short-maturity option pricing.

This sits at the intersection of stochastic analysis, numerical analysis, and mathematical finance -- three traditions that I think are most powerful when used together.

## Broader Interests

I am drawn to a small set of mathematical structures that recur across seemingly distant fields. The thread that ties most of my interests together is **functional analysis** -- particularly Hilbert space methods and operator theory.

A few specific directions I find compelling:

- **Fourier methods for option pricing under Levy processes** -- Carr-Madan FFT, Lewis contour integrals, and the role of the characteristic function as a unifying object.
- **Time series analysis through an operator-theoretic lens** -- autocovariance operators, spectral methods, and connections to ergodic theory.
- **Connections between quantum mechanics and quantitative finance** -- both can be read as theories of expectation values of operators on Hilbert spaces; the philosophical and technical analogies are deeper than usually acknowledged.
- **Green's functions and integral equations** -- recurring objects from PDE to mathematical physics to financial pricing kernels.

## Mathematical Foundations

My work draws on a background in **real and functional analysis, measure theory, Fourier analysis, complex analysis, and probability theory**. I view these not as separate subjects but as a single language, and I am increasingly interested in the **philosophy of mathematics** that underlies the choice of language: questions about Platonism, structuralism, and the unreasonable effectiveness of certain abstract structures in describing the world.
